    • Early Life

  • Young Wolves are called Pups.
  • A single litter usually consists of 1 to 11 pups.
  • Wolf pups are born blind and deaf.
  • Wolf pups become hunters after only 6 months.
  • They are considered adults at 2 years old.
  • Young wolves stay in their parents' pack for at least two years before some of them take off to join other packs or to start their own.

    • Personality

  • Wolves prefer to travel in packs.
  • Wolves are highly social.
  • Wolves Are Family Animals.
  • When a wolf wants to play, it prances about happily and bows
  • Wolves care for each other as individuals. They form friendships and nurture their own sick and injured.
  • All wolves have individual personalities.
  • Wolves have an extremely cooperative personality

    • Features

  • Wolves howl to communicate with other members of the pack.
  • Wolves have 42 teeth.
  • Wolves can run in short bursts at up to 35 miles per hour.
  • They can hear up to six miles away in the forest.
  • A wolf's jaw has a crushing power of nearly 1500 pounds per square inch.

    • Population

  • On average, a wolf will live four to eight years in the wild.
  • There are thought to be only 20 to 80 red wolves currently living in the wild.
  • The Ethiopian wolf is listed as endangered by the IUCN.
  • Wolves were the first animals to be place on the US Endangered Species Act list in 1973.
  • Wolves were mistakenly viewed as a pest species and almost exterminated.
  • Many projects are being carried out to reintroduce many wolf species such as the USFWS in North America and the International Wolf Centre.

    • Facts

  • To help with the red wolf population, wild wolves are given pups that are born in captivity. This is called "fostering."
